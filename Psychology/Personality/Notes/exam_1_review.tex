\section{Chapter 1 -- What is Personality?}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \cyanit{Definition of personality}: Consistent behavior patterns and intrapersonal processes.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Consistent behavior patterns: Behavior that is predictable and stable over time.
        \item Intrapersonal processes: Internal experiences that are consistent over time.
    \end{itemize}
    \item Six theories of personality:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item \cyanit{Psychoanalytic approach}: Focuses on innate needs and unconscious behaviors.
        \item \cyanit{Trait approach}: Emphasizes stable characteristics of individuals.
        \item \cyanit{Biological approach}: Considers inherited predispositions in personality.
        \item \cyanit{Humanistic approach}: Highlights personal growth and self-actualization.
        \item \cyanit{Behavioral/social learning approach}: Focuses on learned behaviors from environment.
        \item \cyanit{Cognitive approach}: Examines mental processes influencing personality.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
