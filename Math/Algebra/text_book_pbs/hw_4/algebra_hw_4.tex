\section*{Section 8}

% Exercises 1, 2, 5, 6, 8, 12, 20, 24, 25, 38*

In Exercises 1, 2, and 5, compute the indicated product involving the following permutations in \(S_{6}\):
\[
    \sigma = \begin{pmatrix}
        1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
        3 & 1 & 4 & 5 & 6 & 2
    \end{pmatrix}, \quad \tau = \begin{pmatrix}
        1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
        2 & 4 & 1 & 3 & 6 & 5
    \end{pmatrix}, \quad \mu = \begin{pmatrix}
        \ldots
    \end{pmatrix}
\]

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \(\tau\sigma\)
    \sol{
        \[
            \tau\sigma = \begin{pmatrix}
                1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
                1 & 2 & 3 & 6 & 5 & 4
            \end{pmatrix}
        \]
    }

    \item \(\tau^{2}\sigma\)
    \sol{
        \[
            \tau\tau\sigma = \begin{pmatrix}
                1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
                2 & 4 & 1 & 5 & 6 & 3
            \end{pmatrix}
        \]
    }

    \setcounter{enumi}{4}
    \item \(\sigma^{-1}\tau\sigma\)
    \sol{
        \[
            \sigma^{-1}\tau\sigma = \begin{pmatrix}
                1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
                2 & 6 & 1 & 5 & 4 & 3
            \end{pmatrix}
        \]
    }
\end{enumerate}


In Exercises 6 and 8, compute the expressions shown for the permutations \(\sigma\), \(\tau\), and \(\mu\) defined prior to Exercise 1.
\begin{enumerate}
    \setcounter{enumi}{5}
    \item \(\abs{\gen{\sigma}}\)
    \sol{
        To find the order of \(\sigma\), we need to determine the smallest positive integer \(k\) such that \(\sigma^k\) is the identity permutation. Thus,
        \[
        \begin{split}
            \sigma = \begin{pmatrix}
                1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
                3 & 1 & 4 & 5 & 6 & 2
            \end{pmatrix}, \quad \sigma^{2} = \begin{pmatrix}
                1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
                4 & 3 & 5 & 6 & 2 & 1
            \end{pmatrix}, \quad \sigma^{3} = \begin{pmatrix}
                1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
                5 & 4 & 6 & 2 & 1 & 3
            \end{pmatrix}
        \end{split}
        \]
        \[
            \sigma^{4} = \begin{pmatrix}
                1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
                6 & 5 & 2 & 1 & 3 & 4
            \end{pmatrix}, \quad \sigma^{5} = \begin{pmatrix}
                1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
                2 & 6 & 1 & 3 & 4 & 5
            \end{pmatrix}, \quad \sigma^{6} = \begin{pmatrix}
                1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
                1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6
            \end{pmatrix}
        \]
        Therefore, the order of \(\sigma\) is 6, so \(\abs{\gen{\sigma}} = 6\).
    }

    \setcounter{enumi}{7}
    \item \(\sigma^{100}\)
    \sol{
        From the last problem, we know that \(\sigma\) has order 6. So, \(\sigma^{100} = \sigma^{4}\). We also found that 
        \[
            \sigma^{4} = \begin{pmatrix}
                1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 \\
                6 & 5 & 2 & 1 & 3 & 4
            \end{pmatrix}.
        \]
    }
\end{enumerate}

Let \(A\) be a set and let \(\sigma \in S_{A}\). For a fixed \(a \in A\), the set
\[
